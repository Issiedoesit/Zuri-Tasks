Katherine Johnson, née Coleman

[August 26, 1918 - February 24, 2020]

Notable aerospace technologist, mathematician, mother and wife



“I don't have a feeling of inferiority. Never had. I'm as good as anybody, but no better.”

Early Life

Katherine Johnson born Creola Katherine Coleman in White Sulphur Springs, Greenbrier County in West Virginia. She was the youngest of four children of Joshua Coleman, a lumberjack, handyman and farmer and his wife, Joylette Coleman.

Her astounding acumen as a mathematician was beginning to get noticed and she graduated from the eighth grade at just 10 years old - for reference this was 3 to 4 years earlier than the average child would and by 13 was attending high school at the West Virginia state college.

Education

Greenbrier County whee Katherine lived at the time didn't offer schooling for African-American students past eighth grade and so the family had to have their daughters attend high school in Institute within the West Virginia State College campus.

She was attending high school at the age of 10 and graduated at 14 and began college at West Virginia State College graduating at 18 Summa cum laude with a bachelors in Mathematics and French .

She attended graduate school for a graduate math program for a year before she left to start her family.

Career

She began work as a teacher after college graduation in 1937 at a black public school but left to enroll in the graduate program at West Virginia University and returned to teaching after the birth of her daughters.

In 1952, a relative informed her about job openings at NACA which later became NASA in 1958 and she applied and began work at the Langley Laboratory in NACA in 1953.

She worked as a human computer in a group of ladies called " the West computers" but would later become the go-to person to for calculations within the organization.

She worked at NASA for 33 years and retired in 1986.

Family

She got married to her first husband, James Goble in 1939 and quit her job as a teacher to enroll in a graduate math program. She later left the program a year later to focus on her family. She had three children with James Goble: Constance, Joylette and Katherine. James Francis Goble was diagosed of brain tumor which was unoperable and he died in 1956.

Three years later, she married James A. "Jim" Johnson, an army veteran and they were together till his death 60 years later in March 2019. He was aged 93.

TIMELINE

Notable Achievement at a Glance

  • 1932: She graduated from high school at the age of 14, matriculated at West Virginia State College (WVSC), an historically black college. She took every course in mathematics offered by the College that her Professor had to add new mathematical courses just for her.

  • 1937: She graduated Summa cum laude - highest honours - from the West Virginia State College, with degrees in mathematics and French, at age 18 and was a member of Alpha Kappa Alpha (a sorority on campus).

  • 1939: She was handpicked by West Virginia State's predident, Dr John W. Davis to be one of three black students to integrate the graduate school at the state's flagship school, West Virginia University and was the only female of the three.

  • 1953: She began work in National Aerospace ans Space Administration (NASA) known then as National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA).

  • 1960 She co-authored a paper on calculations for placing a spacecraft on the moon and t was the first time a woman would recieve credit as an author on a research report, authored and co-authored 25 more scientific papers throughout her lifetime.

  • 1961: Her calculations of orbital mechanics as a NASA employee were critical to the success of the first(Freedom Z) and subsequent U.S. crewed spaceflights.

  • 1969: She worked on calculations that helped synch Project Apollo's Lunar Module with the lunar-orbiting Command and Service Module.

  • 1972 She worked on the Space Shuttle and the Earth Resources Technology Satellite (ERTS, later renamed Landsat)

  • 1986 She retired from NASA after 33 years of work. She was 68 years.

  • 2015: She was awarded a Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2015 by President Barack Obama.

  • 2016: The movie 'Hidden Figure' were she was played by Taraji P. Henson was released about her time at NASA. the Racial and Gender seggregation and how her hardwork and competence began to get acknowledged within NASA.

  • 2020: She was a wife, mother, sister, daughter, grandmother and great grandmother who lived a fulfilling family and career life until her death at 93 in February 2020.
